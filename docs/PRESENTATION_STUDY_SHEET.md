# Refinery Outages — Prep Notes

2027 capacity offline by unit, against 2023–2026 (peak-month, kbd). The booked 2027 plan is lighter
than the 2023–24 highs on every unit, but **heavier than 2026 — the cat cracker most of all** — and it lands
on the **thinnest inventory cushion in years**. 2027 is planned-only and non-Exxon H2 is still filling in, so
the recorded totals are a floor that rises. EIA stock levels are as of the week ending **Jun 19, 2026**.

---

## Gasoline — CDU + FCC

Crude and the cat cracker both feed the gasoline pool, so when they're down gasoline tightens — crude cuts the
whole barrel, the FCC cuts cat gasoline directly. In 2027 the cat cracker is the standout, and the work clusters
in the spring, landing into the summer-grade switchover on a thin cushion.

- **Crude (CDU) peak offline:** 2023 ~2,180 · 2024 ~2,140 · 2025 ~1,860 · 2026 ~1,410 · **2027 ~1,310 booked**
  (peaks October). Booked H1 is **+7% vs 2026**.
- **Cat cracker (FCC) peak offline:** 2023 ~1,030 · 2024 ~1,050 · 2025 ~700 · 2026 ~560 · **2027 ~450 booked**
  (peaks February). Booked H1 is **+66% vs 2026** — the most elevated unit.
- **Driving it:** crude — Joliet (250, Midwest, Apr–May), Cherry Point (250, West, Mar), Norco (240, Gulf, fall);
  cat cracker — Baytown (125) and Beaumont (120), both Gulf in Q1, plus Catlettsburg (98, Midwest, fall).
  Gulf-heavy, clustered Feb–May.
- **EIA:** gasoline stocks **−5% vs the 5-yr average**, demand ~8.8 mbpd (−3% YoY). The spring turnarounds hit
  right as summer-grade demand builds (switchover March–June).

## Diesel / Distillate — CDU + Hydrocracker

Distillate tightness is mostly crude and inventories — the hydrocracker is small in kbd. Every crude turnaround
cuts distillate yield along with gasoline, and distillate stocks are the tightest of the three commodities.

- **Crude (CDU):** same as above — crude down cuts distillate too. 2027 booked ~1,310 peak, **+7% H1 vs 2026**,
  Gulf-led.
- **Hydrocracker peak offline:** 2023 ~490 · 2024 ~430 · 2025 ~255 · 2026 ~170 · **2027 ~140 booked**
  (peaks February). Booked H1 **+9% vs 2026** — modest, front-loaded in Q1 with a fall window.
- **Driving it:** hydrocracker — Beaumont (65), Galveston Bay (60), Houston/Valero (50), all Gulf, Q1 plus Sep–Oct.
- **EIA:** distillate stocks **−10% vs the 5-yr average — the tightest of the three.** Thin cushion heading into
  winter heating and diesel demand.

## Chem-Feed — Reformer + Naphtha

Reformers run naphtha into reformate (the octane in gasoline) and aromatics/petrochemical feed. Crude turnarounds
pull naphtha supply off faster than reformer turnarounds free it, so naphtha runs short — bullish octane. The
window here is the rest of 2026 plus 2027.

- **Reformer peak offline:** 2023 ~530 · 2024 ~840 · 2025 ~440 · 2026 ~210 · **2027 ~185 booked** (peaks March).
  Booked H1 **+39% vs 2026**.
- **Naphtha balance:** 2027 is short in **11 of 12 months** (net −1,347 kbd; tightest October and September);
  2026 short in **10 of 12** (tightest April and February). Crude down > reformer down → octane bid.
- **Driving it:** reformer — Whiting (60, Midwest, fall), Joliet (52, Midwest, spring), Norco (40, Gulf, spring).
  Midwest and Gulf.
- **EIA:** gasoline **−5%** — octane is scarce exactly when reformers are down, so the reformate loss tightens the
  blend pool just as summer-grade needs octane.

## Month-over-month (latest)

Total offline fell ~275 kbd from June to July 2026 (1,350 → 1,075) as turnarounds wound down into driving season.
PADD 3 (Gulf) drove the drop; the cat cracker and crude led by unit. 5 outages newly appeared, 48 came back online.
("July" is the latest month in the pull, not a forecast.)

## Operator color

**Garyville (Marathon, Gulf / PADD 3).** Multiple crude trains; Marathon perennially books one down but **always
leaves one running**, and has repeatedly delayed on strong margins — real chance it **slips to 2028** (out of the
2027 book). The two ~145-kbd Sep–Nov entries are indicative H2, not confirmed — plan for at most one train, possibly
none in 2027.

**Robinson (Marathon, Midwest / PADD 2) — confirmed out.** 2027 is a **small, reformer-led turnaround — the
Ultraformer (reformer) and the diesel hydrotreater, not the crude unit**, which stays up. But Robinson is in
**PADD 2, which is poorly connected** (few pipeline options to move intermediates around), so with the reformer
down they **may have to cut crude runs anyway** — so treat it as a possible **soft crude reduction in the Midwest
even though the crude unit isn't formally in turnaround**. (The big Robinson crude turnaround was 2026, not 2027.)
